Mercury is the smallest planet of the Solar System and the closest to the Sun.

With a diameter of about 4,880 kilometers and a mass of 0.055 of Earth's mass and is orbiting the Sun within 87.969 days. Mercury has the highest value for an orbital anomaly of all the planets of the solar system, it has the smallest axial tilt of these planets and it completes three orbits around its axis for every two orbital cycles. The perihelion changes in Mercury's orbit in its orbital motion at a rate of 43 arcminutes in each century. Mercury appears brilliantly when the observer sees it from Earth, and its apparent magnitude ranges between -2.3 to 5.7, but it is not easy to see from high latitudes while it can be seen from medium and low latitudes, especially when it has a maximum elongation relative to the Sun, which is 28.3 degrees before dawn or after sunset. Reaching Mercury poses many technical challenges, as the planet is very close to the Sun, and the spacecraft launched from Earth must travel a distance of 91 million kilometers towards the Sun And her attractiveness Mercury also has an orbital speed equivalent to 48 kilometers per second, while the Earth has an orbital speed of 30 km per second, so the spacecraft must change its speed dramatically to be able to be able to enter the transition Hehman orbit near Mercury, Mercury is a very small planet, and because of this its mass and therefore its gravity is much less than having a significant atmosphere, in addition to its proximity to the sun and its extreme temperature make the escape of its atmosphere quick and easy. However, it has a thin external atmosphere.

Consisting of hydrogen, helium, oxygen, sodium, calcium, potassium, and some other elements. The “exosphere” or “exosphere” is the upper layer of the atmosphere, but its smallness on Mercury and the lack of other layers makes astronomers consider that it does not have an important atmosphere compared to other planets But the atoms of its outer shell are not stable, they constantly escape its gravity (mainly because of the solar wind), and then are replaced by others from various sources such as the solar wind itself, ash, and debris thrown from the surface due to collisions. In 2008, water vapor was discovered in the atmosphere of Mercury, and is believed to be the result of the prevention of hydrogen and oxygen atoms in the atmosphere. Hydrogen and helium atoms may come to its atmosphere from the solar wind, where Mercury temporarily captures it before escaping back into space due to its poor gravity. And there are no clouds, wind, or any other weather phenomena on Mercury.

In its early days after its birth 4.6 billion years ago, Mercury had an atmosphere, but shortly after its birth, its atmosphere eroded and disappeared due to the strong solar wind blowing on it due to its extreme proximity to the sun. The Mariner 10 spacecraft detected very small amounts of helium at an altitude of 1,000 km above Mercury's surface during its flight near it in 1974 and 1975, and Mariner 10 data is also the first to prove the existence of a thin outer shell of Mercury, Mercury has no natural moons, and he and Venus are the only two planets that do not have a satellite system. To answer the reason for the anomaly of these two planets, a hypothesis was proposed in the mid-1960s that Mercury was a moon of Venus and managed to escape its orbit around Venus
